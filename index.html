Favour O. Igwezeke

I am a final-year PharmD student at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka, working at the intersection of machine learning and biology. My research focuses on causal machine learning for biological mechanism discovery — building ML systems that reason about biological mechanisms rather than exploiting statistical patterns, and developing principled methods to verify that distinction.

I work with Assoc. Prof. Collins A. Onyeto (Dept. of Pharmacology & Toxicology, UNN) on my thesis, and collaborate with the CaresAI Research Group (PI: Dr. Mary Adewunmi), the SPARK MICCAI Academy, and the African Society for Bioinformatics & Computational Biology / NIH ODSS. I am grateful to be supported by the ISMB 2026 Conference Fellowship (ISCB) and the Okwuchukwu Fund Research Award, UNN.

Previously I interned at Genomac Institute Inc. in cancer genomics and at HackBio in next-generation sequencing bioinformatics.

Research Interests

I am broadly interested in machine learning for biology and biomedical discovery. On the machine learning side, I think about causal and mechanistic representation learning, model evaluation under distribution shift, and uncertainty-aware reasoning in biomedical AI. On the biology side, I am drawn to single-cell genomics, trajectory inference, perturbation modelling, drug response prediction, and antibody repertoire analysis.
